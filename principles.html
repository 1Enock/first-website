Principles of Quantum Mechanics

This is a brief overview of the key principles of quantum mechanics.

Quantum mechanics has specific principles that govern particles's behaviour within the quantum world

Wave-Particle Duality

This duality is best illustrated by the famous double-slit experiment, where particles such as electrons create an interference pattern when not observed, but behave like particles when observed.

Uncertainty Principle

This principle highlights the inherent limitations of measurement in quantum mechanics and has profound implications for our understanding of the microscopic world.

Superposition

Superposition states that quantum particles can exist in two states at the same time until they are measured.

It is hypothesised using schrondingers cat paradox.

Schrondinger is one of the pioneers of quantum mechanics

Entanglement

Entanglement is where particles become interconnected and the state of one particle

One can instantaneously affect the state of another, regardless of distance.
